How Stars Age:

Stars go through a life cycle that can last for millions or even billions of years. They begin as large clouds of gas and dust called nebulae. Gravity pulls this material together until it becomes hot and dense enough for nuclear fusion to begin, creating a new star. During most of its life, a star spends its time converting hydrogen into helium and producing light and heat.

As a star gets older, it starts to run out of hydrogen fuel. Smaller stars, like the Sun, expand into red giants before shedding their outer layers and becoming white dwarfs. Much larger stars become red supergiants and eventually explode in a supernova. After the explosion, they may become either a neutron star or a black hole, depending on their mass.

The way stars age is important because it explains how the universe changes over time. When stars die, they release elements such as carbon, oxygen, and iron into space. These elements are used to form new stars, planets, and even living things, making the life cycle of stars essential to the development of the universe.
